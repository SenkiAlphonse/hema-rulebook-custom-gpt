# DIS Penalty Core — Fegyelmi Büntetések (Referee Anchor)

This file is a strict anchor for disciplinary escalation.
Cards are the formal procedure. Do not replace them with vague “verbal warning ladders”.

---

## 1) Core Principle: Cards are the Procedure

### **The classification of penalties** - The rules distinguish Fencing related and Conduct related offences:
See: DIS-3.1.1 (and following entries)

- **Víváshoz kötődő büntetések, amelyek a vívás közben elkövetett vétségek esetén alkalmazhatóak** 
(Disciplinary penalties (order, sportsmanship, misconduct)):
    - területvesztés
    - a ténylegesen elért találat meg nem adása,
    - a ténylegesen nem kapott találat megítélése,
    - a versenyszámból való kizárás.
    Reference: DIS-3.1.1.1

- **Fegyelmi büntetések, amelyek a rend, a fegyelem és a sportszellem ellen elkövetett vétségek esetén alkalmazhatóak**:
    - Ténylegesen nem kapott találat odaítélése
    - versenyszámból való kizárás
    - A teljes bajnokságból való kizárás
    - A verseny helyszínéről való kiutasítás
    - a Szövetség vagy az adott Szervezőcsapat által szervezett rendezvényekről meghatározott időre történő kizárás
    Reference: DIS-3.1.1.2

**The competition uses a formal lap (card) -based penalty system**:

The lapok (cards) represent groups of penalties:
- **Sárga Lap (Yellow Card) = official warning**
- **Piros Lap (Red Card) = penalty hit**
- **Fekete Lap (Black Card) = exclusion / suspension**
- Penalties accumulate within the bout (black has tournament-level effects).

See DIS-4.1.5, DIS-4.3.17.
---

## 2) Sárga Lap (Yellow Card) = Warning (Not “Verbal First”)

### DIS-4.1.5.1 — Sárga Lap (Yellow Card) (Warning)

A warning is expressed by showing a **Sárga Lap (Yellow Card)**.
After this, **any new offence** results in a penalty hit.

- Yellow is the formal warning mechanism.

Reference: DIS-4.1.5.1

---

## 3) Piros Lap (Red Card) = Penalty Hit + Escalation Lock

### DIS-4.1.5.2 — Piros Lap (Red Card) (Penalty Hit)

A penalty hit is expressed by showing a **Piros Lap (Red Card)**.
The opponent gains one point.

Important escalation rule:

- After a Piros Lap (Red Card), only another red or a black may follow,
  depending on the nature of the next offence, and even if the next offence belongs to the first group.

Reference: DIS-4.1.5.2

---

## 4) Fekete Lap (Black Card) = Exclusion

### DIS-4.1.5.3 — Fekete Lap (Black Card)

Fekete Lap (Black Card) means exclusion from the competition / suspension from the remainder of the tournament.

Reference: DIS-4.1.5.3

---

## 5) Vétségek csoportjai (Offence Groups) Control Escalation

- Disciplinary and fencing related offences are classified into **four groups**.
- Ha a versenybíró olyan vívót büntet, aki egyidejűleg több vétséget követett el, először a kevésbé súlyos hiba büntetését kell kiszabni.

### Group 1 — First offence starts at Yellow

- First Group: first offence = **Yellow**
- Repeated offence in the same bout = **Red each time**
- If the fencer already has a red from Group 2 or 3,
  then even their first Group 1 offence becomes another red.

Reference: DIS-4.1.9

---

### Group 2 — Immediate Red (No Yellow Required)

All Group 2 offences are punished with **Piros Lap (Red Card) from the first offence**.

Reference: DIS-4.3.11

---

### Group 3 — Immediate Red, then Black if repeated

- First offence = **Red**
- Repeated Group 3 offence in the same bout = **Black**

Reference: DIS-4.3.12–DIS-4.3.13

---

### Group 4 — Immediate Black

Any Group 4 offence is punished immediately with **Fekete Lap (Black Card)**.

Reference: DIS-4.3.15

---

## 6) Verbal Warning is Optional, Not the System

The rules mention that the Referee may apply penalties with or without **verbal warning**,
but this does not replace the formal card procedure.

Use verbal intervention only for borderline, discretionary conduct management.

When an offence is clearly regulated and categorized by the Rules, Referees must apply the correct category. 
Using unofficial verbal warnings when a specific penalty is prescribed, causes an impactful offence to go without consequences, thus it can hurt the principle of equal and just procedure.  

---

## 7) Practical Application: Persistent Yelling

Persistent yelling may be:

- normal emotional expression (not automatically an offence)
- or disorderly/disruptive conduct affecting order

The referee must stop disruptive conduct immediately.

Reference: DIS-1.2.3

Escalation should follow the offence group + card procedure:

- borderline → optional short request
- clear disruption → Yellow (Group 1) or higher if severe
- intimidation/serious misconduct → Group 3 or Group 4 escalation
